About F1

Formula 1® racing began in 1950 and is the world’s most prestigious motor racing competition, as well as the world’s most popular annual sporting series: The 2019 FIA Formula One World Championship™ runs from March to December and spans 21 races in 21 countries across four continents. Formula One World Championship Limited is part of Formula 1® and holds the exclusive commercial rights to the FIA Formula One World Championship™.

FIA

Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA) is the governing body responsible for overseeing the rules, regulations, and overall management of the sport. Established in 1904, the FIA's primary mission is to ensure fairness, safety, and sustainability in motorsport, including Formula 1, its flagship competition.

Key Roles of the FIA in Formula 1

  1. Regulatory Authority: The FIA creates and enforces rules for car design, race procedures, and penalties.
  2. Safety Oversight: It implements safety measures like the Halo device and improved circuit designs.
  3. Race Management: The FIA manages race weekends, appointing officials and overseeing events.
  4. Promoting Sustainability: It supports initiatives like hybrid engines and sustainable fuels.
  5. Licensing and Classification: The FIA issues licenses to drivers and approves circuits for F1 races.
  6. Regulating Championships: It ensures fair competition and oversees the annual Formula 1 World Championship.

Historical Impact

Over the years, the FIA has evolved Formula 1 into one of the most advanced and widely followed sports, balancing entertainment, safety, and technological progress.
